#Compute correlation matrix
cor_matrix <- cor(cancer[, 3:9])
#Create a heatmap of the correlation matrix
heatmap(cor_matrix, main = "Heatmap of Correlation Matrix")

  1. How are all the independent variables related to each other? Heatmap is useful when we want to show relationship between multiple variables. First we calculate correlation matrix and then display it as a heatmap. Each variable shows the relationship itself and all other variables. Weve computed heatmap for all the independent variables here. The light colour shows low correlation and darker colour shows higher correlation. Patterns may include clusters of similar colors, gradients, or changes in color intensity. These patterns may indicate relationships, trends, or clusters in the data. For eg, we can say that coughing and shortness of breath are closely related since they lie on a darker scale.
